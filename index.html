DREAMSTATE

A society modeled around one activity - dreaming, advertised as a utopia, but practiced as an authoritarian state. The citizens (dreamers) desire to escape reality ridden by scarcity, yet still remain under the grasps of the elite and their monopoly on leisure.

The Dreamers

Duties

The Dreamers dream for 23 hours a day and create for an hour, completing a whole day. This keeps Dreamers under control and fosters creativity.

Social Class

Facebook perceives dreamers as test subjects and disposable therefore their social class is low.

Life Span

Researchers have found that a dreamers creativity diminishes as they start reaching a certain age for many it is thirty-five years. 

Resource Use

 Facebook regulates the resources dreamers receive because they want to be effective and economical.

The Default State

Reality

Modular Buildings

Buildings can be stacked freely for communal and modular living, allowing Facebook to cut down on spending and increase adaptability of structures.

Transformable Buildings

Dreamers are able to mold the buildings to their desires with the guidance of an AI. Researchers have found that dreamers often express their creativity by reshaping buildings.

Facebook Propaganda

Facebook, who funded this scientific project, utilizes every building in the default state as an opportunity to display their propaganda. In order to recruit people, they marketed the Dream State as a utopia. They continue this perception by branding buildings with their logo. 

Dark and Bleak Landscape

This is a representation of a street block, which utilizes concrete and gray colors, as an illustration of how dreamers often perceive the default state as a secluded place. The default state was originally not intended to be that way but researchers did not have enough funds to fully develop the default state. Researchers are studying how the default state may be having impacts on dreamers’ creativity. 

Distorted Skyscraper

In default state, buildings are often distorted to convey the feeling of abnormality. They also take a bleak and dark theme that invokes and enforces the isolation dreamers often experience.
